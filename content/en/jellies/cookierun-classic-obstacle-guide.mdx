## Understanding the Obstacles

The game introduces a variety of obstacles designed to test your skills and reflexes. Here's a breakdown of the common types you'll face:

- **Short Obstacles**: These require a single jump to clear.
- **Tall Obstacles**: These necessitate a double jump to bypass.
- **Hanging Obstacles**: These can be tricky to slide under.
- **Small Flying Obstacles**: Quick reactions are needed to avoid these.

In **Special Episodes**, the challenge is heightened with obstacles that deal out double damage or unique mechanics that require precise timing and strategy.

## Navigating Obstacles: Basic Knowledge

To successfully navigate these obstacles, it's important to understand the basic mechanics of movement in 'CookieRun Classic':

- **Jumping Once**: The standard way to clear short and tall obstacles.
- **Double Jump**: A powerful move to clear obstacles that require more height.
- **Sliding**: A useful technique to avoid hanging obstacles.
- **Power Jellies**: Special jellies that can help you bypass obstacles or increase your energy.

In **Episode 2: Primeval Jungle**, a new Power Jelly is introduced that can destroy obstacles, adding an extra layer of strategy to your gameplay.

## Jelly Trails: A Guide Through the Obstacles

Jelly trails in 'CookieRun Classic' serve as both a guide and a challenge. They are placed strategically to hint at incoming obstacles or provide alternate paths. However, they can also be deceptive, requiring players to use their skills to navigate effectively.

## Double Jumping and Rapid Double Jump: Advanced Techniques

For more advanced players, mastering the double jump and rapid double jump can be the difference between success and failure. These techniques allow you to clear obstacles that would otherwise be insurmountable. However, they require precise timing and practice.

### Double Jumping Hanging Obstacles

Despite their appearance, hanging obstacles can actually be double-jumped. This technique is particularly useful in special episodes like **Ghost Pirate's Island of Coins**. It requires the player to know the exact moment when the second jump can be made.

### Rapid Double Jump

Also known as "double-tapping," this technique involves tapping the jump area twice at the same time. It's especially useful in floors of the **Tower of Frozen Waves** and with certain Cookies like the Lime Cookie, Roll Cake Cookie, and Skating Queen Cookie. However, it requires precise timing to avoid collisions with hanging obstacles.

## FAQ

**Q: How many energy points do I lose when hitting an obstacle?**
A: As of the **Wrath of the Dragon update**, hitting most obstacles costs you 40 energy points. Small flying obstacles, however, cost 20 energy points.

**Q: Can I double-jump hanging obstacles?**
A: Yes, you can double-jump hanging obstacles, but it requires precise timing and is particularly useful in special episodes like **Ghost Pirate's Island of Coins**.

**Q: What are Power Jellies, and how do they help?**
A: Power Jellies are special jellies that can help you bypass obstacles or increase your energy. In **Episode 2: Primeval Jungle**, a new Power Jelly is introduced that can destroy obstacles.

**Q: How can I improve my double-tapping technique?**
A: Improving your double-tapping technique requires practice. Try to tap the jump area at the same time using your thumb and index finger, and practice on different types of obstacles to get a feel for the timing.
